    Memex Documentation#

    Warning

    This guide is a work in progress and is not complete yet. We are actively working on adding more content and information.

    Welcome to Memex#

    Memex is a High-Performance Computing (HPC) cluster, operated by the Carnegie Research Computing Center to provide computing resources to Carnegie Science researchers across all of its campuses. This documentation includes FAQs, hardware details, installed software, as well as information on user training.

    Why use Memex?#

    Memex is a unified resource for your work and has many advantages over individual or department-only solutions: it is hosted in a 24/7 datacenter, which includes secure access, backup power, and access to Stanford's remote hands service. All of these features provide the basis for any scientific computing solution.

    Memex users benefit from performance levels that only large scale HPC systems can offer: high-performance I/O infrastructure, petabytes of storage, large variety of hardware configurations, GPU accelerators, centralized system administration and management provided by the Carnegie Research Computing Center (CRCC).

    Memex allows researchers and collaborators to avoid the cost of operating their own cluster while getting access to a larger, professionally managed computing resource.

    How much does it cost?#

    Memex is free to use for anyone doing sponsored research at Carnegie. Any faculty member can request access for research purposes, and get an account with a base storage allocation and compute time on the global and department pool of resources.

    In case those free resources are not sufficient, Carnegie Research Computing provides faculty with the opportunity to purchase additional resources. whether it is on-prem hardware or cloud computing, for their lab members. Using a traditional compute cluster condominium model, participating faculty and their teams get priority access to the resources they purchase. When they're idle, those resources are available to use by other owners on the cluster, giving them access to virtually unlimited resources.

    Acknowledgment / citation#

    It is important and expected that publications resulting from computations performed on Memex acknowledge this. The following wording is suggested:

    Acknowledgment

    Some of the computing for this project was performed on the Memex cluster. We would like to thank Carnegie Institution for Science and the Carnegie Sci-Comp Committee for providing computational resources and support that contributed to these research results.

    Support#

    Email (recommended)#

    Research Computing support can be reached by sending an email to memexsupport@carnegiescience.edu and mentioning Memex.

    How to submit effective support requests

    To ensure a timely and relevant response, please make sure to include some additional details, such as job ids, commands executed and error messages received, so we can help you better. For more details, see the Troubleshooting page.

    As a member of the Memex community, you're also automatically subscribed to:

    • the memex-announce mailing-list, which is only used by the HPC team to send important announcements about Memex,
    • the memex-discuss mailing list1, which is used to connect Memex users together. If you have a general question about software used on Memex, want to reach out to the other Memex users to share tips and good practices, tutorials or other info, please feel free to do so through this mailing-list.

    Office hours#

    We can't accommodate walk-ins

    We're unfortunately not staffed to accommodate walk-ins, so please reach out through out Slack Channel,#hpc.

    Sending a question to memexsupport@carnegiescience.edu is always the best first option for questions. That way you can include detailed descriptions of the problem or question, valuable output and error messages and any steps you took when you encountered your error. Also, everyone on our team will see your ticket, enabling the most appropriate group member to respond.

    Using Slack is a good place for more generalized questions about HPC, Slurm, Linux usage, data storage, queue structures/scheduling, job optimization and general capabilities of Memex. It's also useful for more technically nuanced questions that may not be easily answered with our ticketing system. In office hours some problems can indeed be solved quickly or progress can be made so that you can then work self-sufficiently towards a solution on your own.

    What to expect

    • We can rarely help with application-specific or algorithm problems.

    • You should plan your projects sufficiently in advance and not reach out at the last minute, right before a deadline. Memex is a fairly busy resource with about 50 active users at any given time and you should not expect your jobs to complete before a given date.

    • Not all questions and problems can be solved or answered via Slack, especially ones involving hardware, filesystem or network issues.

    Office hours are held during normal business hours and staff can be reached directly via Slack for an informal text or video chat:

    • Monday-Friday, from 10am to 3pm EST

    Quick Start#

    If you're in a rush, here's a 3-step ultra-quick start:

    1. connect to Memex 

      $ ssh -XY username@memex.carnegiescience.edu

    2. get an interactive session on a compute node 

      [bash ~]$ srun -N1 --pty bash -i

    3. run a command 

      [username@memex-c001 ~]$ module load python/2.7.0
[username@memex-c001 ~]$ python -c "print('Hello Memex')"
Hello Memex

    Congrats! You ran your first job on Memex!
